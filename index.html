Research Interests

Quantum Information and Quantum Computing

The XXI century is about to experience a technological revolution like no other before. A revolution based on quantum mechanics. Quantum computing and quantum information are a new paradigm in the way we look at and process information. You may have had heard words like "Qubit," "Quantum Parallelism," "Quantum Supremacy," etc. Those concepts arise when we realize that information, just like atoms, is physical! Hence, we can infuse a bit of quantumness into the ideas of information and computation. Also, not is people trying to construct "Quantum Software," they are also trying to build "Quantum Hardware!" As you see, there are a lot of exciting ideas that are being developed right now. As an eager enthusiast of the field, I started in 2016 a series of workshops entitled "Quantum Chronicles" to teach young students about the upcoming quantum revolution. Condensed Matter Physics

The physics of large collection of atoms and the study of the emergent properties that arise from their collective behavior is what we call Condensed Matter physics. The beauty of this field of physics is how we can use quantum mechanics to explain macroscopic properties observed in complex materials. Perhaps the first, and one of the more elegant and profound examples of condensed matter physics predictions is Einstein's theoretical analysis of the heat capacity of solids. With very simple assumptions about the behavior of atoms in a crystal, Einstein was able to construct an accurate description of heat at the molecular level. Nowadays, the field has grown so much as to become perhaps the most prominent subfield of physics. Many physicists go as far as to call condensed matter physics "the physics of twenty-one century." The newly discovered exotic properties and phenomena in this field promise to disrupt the current technologies, fostering the Quantum Revolution upon us.

Quantum Many-body Theory

The physics (and math) of many-body systems is very intricate, let it be classical or quantum. Many-body interactions give rise to chaos, phase transitions, and phenomena that only exist as a consequence of the collective behavior of the bodies constituting the system. To make things even more exciting and perplexing, we can have closed and open systems, in equilibrium, or far from it. Given the tremendous challenge that the physics of many-body systems imposes, the field is still developing its foundations. Every year new approaches, methods, and techniques appear to deal, usually, with specific systems. In this field, problems get very complicated, very easily very rapidly! The many-body problem plays a central role not only in condensed matter physics but quantum chemistry as well, where molecules and chemical reactions are indeed many-body quantum systems. Hence, further development in this field will trigger further progress in many multidisciplinary questions. I work on this field using Coupled Cluster theory in molecules, keep reading to know more!

Machine Learning

Machine Learning is more than a black box that gives you (sometimes and of doubt quality) answers to s pecific problems. At its core, it is Information Theory. It is how we recollect data, the different ways in which we process it, fit it, and interpret it. There are out there, pre-trained models and algorithms that facilitate anyone to start playing around and even start a professional career in machine learning and Data Science; however, one can not merely take anyone else model and spect to solve all of our problems. Picture it like this. A professional photographer could give his camera with all the technical settings and lenses that you need to take amazing photos; however, is it reasonable to think that with those tools, anyone can just become a world-class photographer? The answer, of course, is a resounding NO. The professional has something that everyone else lacks, which allows him to find the right angle with the proper exposure, etc. Do you know what that is? Training and Experience! The same applies to data science and machine learning! Right now I am working on two projects which involve machine learning applied to molecular systems and social development. More about those projects soon!
